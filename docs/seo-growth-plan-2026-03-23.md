# Kilogrammes SEO / GEO / AEO / E-E-A-T Growth Plan

Date: 2026-03-23
Website: https://kilogramme-shop.com/
Keyword source: /home/ivan/kilogrammez/keywords_fr.txt

## Executive Summary

- The site has a strong commercial base, solid product/category architecture, and good brand positioning for CBD ecommerce.
- The biggest weakness was not technical indexability. It was content quality, intent fit, and schema coverage on strategic commercial pages.
- The raw keyword list contains 979 entries, but it is not clean. It mixes relevant commercial CBD terms with duplicate strain lookups, foreign-language informational terms, and high-risk THC recipe/drug-intent queries.
- The usable opportunity is concentrated in four revenue-driving areas:
  1. CBD ecommerce and category intent
  2. B2B / grossiste CBD intent
  3. Franchise intent
  4. Informational strain, legality, effects, and education content that supports commercial discovery

### What The Site Is Doing Well

- Strong product/category/shop page foundation already exists.
- Clear commercial paths exist for shop, B2B, franchise, and location pages.
- Product schema, breadcrumb schema, canonical handling, and sitemap generation were already partially in place.

### What Needed To Improve

- Homepage H1 and meta intent were weak for organic search.
- Several strategic pages had thin content and no JSON-LD support.
- The site lacked FAQ/AEO structure on pages with clear People Also Ask potential.
- Category pages lacked collection-style schema support.
- E-E-A-T signals were present in brand language but under-expressed structurally.

### Changes Implemented

- Homepage SEO positioning improved with a real search-oriented H1, stronger metadata, richer bottom-of-page content, and FAQ schema.
- Schema coverage expanded with FAQPage and CollectionPage support.
- Thin commercial pages were expanded with richer trust-building and search-intent-aligned content.
- Metadata across key pages was rewritten to improve relevance and CTR.

## Comprehensive SEO Audit Findings

### A. Content Gaps

#### Missing Topics

- CBD legality by market intent
  Why it matters: repeated search signals around legality and country-specific CBD queries.
- Product education content for strains and effect queries
  Why it matters: repeated Amnesia Haze, Lemon Haze, Purple Haze, Mango Kush and related intent across the list.
- Wholesale and supplier comparison content
  Why it matters: the site has a grossiste page, but the keyword file shows broader supplier and wholesale-intent coverage.
- CBD sleep, weight management, eczema, prostate, difference queries
  Why it matters: these are clear informational-supporting queries suitable for expert-reviewed educational content.

#### Thin / Weak Content

- /grossiste-cbd/
  Before changes, too thin for B2B intent and lacked FAQ structure.
- /franchise-cbd/
  Before changes, attractive visually but thin versus real franchise research behavior.
- /contact/
  Functional but not semantically enriched.
- /affiliation/
  Commercially useful but light on proof and context.
- /safety/
  Good trust topic, but too short to fully support E-E-A-T.

#### Duplicate / Overlapping Risk

- Shop city pages and location-intent pages could overlap if additional geo pages are created without differentiated copy.
- Category pages can overlap with future blog content unless articles target education while categories remain transactional.

### B. SEO Weaknesses

#### Title Tag Issues

- Homepage title previously undersold main commercial terms.
- Several commercial pages were generic and not sufficiently click-worthy.

#### Meta Description Issues

- Several pages were descriptive but not persuasive.
- Some pages lacked strong value props like testing, delivery, breadth, or expertise.

#### Heading Structure Issues

- Homepage originally used a promo-product H1 instead of a site-level search intent H1.
- Thin pages lacked deeper H2/H3 structure aligned to search questions.

#### URL Structure Issues

- Core URL structure is good overall.
- Future blog content needs disciplined slugs grouped by topic, not one-off article naming.

#### Image Optimization Issues

- Product and marketing imagery is visually strong, but many pages still need tighter alt text discipline tied to content purpose rather than decorative phrasing.

### C. Intent Mismatch

- The homepage was selling promotional drops before clearly establishing what the site is, what it sells, and why it is trustworthy.
- Commercial pages were often strong visually but weaker in answer depth for searchers comparing suppliers, franchises, or product categories.

### D. Structural Issues

#### Internal Linking

- Blog-to-commerce linking is underutilized.
- Safety and quality content should link directly into product and category pages.
- B2B and franchise should cross-link through decision-stage anchors.

#### Site Architecture

- The ecommerce architecture is strong.
- The missing layer is educational clustering around products, strains, legality, and usage questions.

#### Technical SEO

- Indexability is generally sound.
- robots.txt excludes key non-index pages correctly.
- Sitemap is generated and build output is healthy.
- Remaining technical work is mostly enhancement, not repair.

### E. E-E-A-T Weaknesses

#### Experience Gaps

- The brand clearly has product and market experience, but process experience is not documented enough in informational content.

#### Expertise Gaps

- No clear editorial authorship layer on educational content.
- Limited expert framing on sensitive informational topics.

#### Authoritativeness Gaps

- Strong brand voice, but too few visible references to testing process, methods, or independent evidence.

#### Trustworthiness Gaps

- Some trust pages exist, but they need stronger integration across the site.
- Sensitive health-adjacent topics should be handled cautiously with verification and clear non-medical framing.
